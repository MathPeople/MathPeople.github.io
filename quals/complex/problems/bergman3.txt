<problem><p>Take a bounded domain \(\Omega\). Let \(\{e_n\}_{n=1}^\infty\) be an orthonormal basis of \(L^2(\Omega)\). For \(z,w\in\Omega\), show</p><p>\[K(z,w)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty e_n(z)\overline{e_n(w)}\]</p><p>Justify that this formulation is independent of choice of basis and show that \(K(z,z)&gt;0\) for any \(z\in\Omega\).</p></problem><solution><p>Take the holomorphic (in \(z\)) function \(K(z,w)\) and express it with respect to the basis. To do this, recall \(K(z,w)=R_w(z)\) for a holomorphic \(L^2\) function \(R_w\).</p><p>\[R_w=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\langle R_w,e_n\rangle e_n=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\overline{\langle e_n,R_w\rangle}e_n\]</p><p>Recall the point of obtaining \(R_w\) in the first place; it is the Riesz representation of the evaluation functional \(L_w\). That is, \(\langle e_n,R_w\rangle=e_n(w)\). Thus</p><p>\[R_w=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\overline{e_n(w)}e_n\]</p><p>Plugging in \(z\) to this function gives</p><p>\[K(z,w)=R_w(z)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\overline{e_n(w)}e_n(z)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty e_n(z)\overline{e_n(w)}\]</p><p>The formula holds regardless of choice of basis because it was obtained from the projection property, which holds for any basis, and from the properties of \(K\), which were derived without the use of bases.</p><p>Using the formula,</p><p>\[K(z,z)=\sum_{n=1}^\infty|e_n(z)|^2\geq0\]</p><p>Moreover, there must be some \(n\) for which \(e_n(z)\neq0\) because \(1\) is a holomorphic \(L^2\) function on \(\Omega\) and</p><p>\[1=\sum_{n=1}^\infty\langle 1,e_n\rangle e_n\]</p><p>In order for this equality to hold at \(z\), not all the \(e_n\) can vanish at \(z\). Thus \(K(z,z)&gt;0\).</p></solution><topics><SequenceOfFunctions/><Conjugation/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
