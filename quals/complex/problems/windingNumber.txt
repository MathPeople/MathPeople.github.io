<problem><p>Let \(\Omega\) be a region and take a loop \(\gamma:[0,1]\to\Omega\). Let \(a\in\Omega\setminus\gamma\). Recall the winding number is</p><p>\[n(\gamma;a)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma\frac{1}{z-a}dz\]</p><p>If \(b\) is in the same path component as \(a\) in \(\Omega\setminus\gamma\), show that \(n(\gamma;a)=n(\gamma;b)\).</p></problem><solution><p>Let \(p:[0,1]\to\Omega\setminus\gamma\) be a path with \(p(0)=a\) and \(p(1)=b\). We want to look at the integral</p><p>\[\int_\gamma\frac{1}{z-p(s)}dz\]</p><p>We will do a change of variables. Let \(\gamma_s(t)=\gamma(t)-p(s)+a\). Note \(\gamma_s(t)\) never hits \(a\) for any \(s,t\) because \(p\) never hits \(\gamma\). Also \(\gamma_s&apos;=\gamma&apos;\) and</p><p>\[\int_\gamma\frac{1}{z-p_s}dz=\int_0^1\frac{\gamma&apos;(t)}{\gamma(t)-p_s}dt=\int_0^1\frac{\gamma_s&apos;(t)}{\gamma_s(t)-a}dt=\int_{\gamma_s}\frac{1}{z-a}dz\]</p><p>We have constructed a path homotopy from \(\gamma_0\) to \(\gamma_1\), where the image of the homotopy lies in \(\mathbb{C}\setminus\{a\}\). Thus, by Cauchy&apos;s integral theorem,</p><p>\[\int_{\gamma_0}\frac{1}{z-a}dz=\int_{\gamma_1}\frac{1}{z-a}dz\]</p><p>Note that \(\gamma_0\) is just \(\gamma\), so the left hand side is \((2\pi i)n(\gamma;a)\). The right hand side is</p><p>\[\int_0^1\frac{\gamma_1&apos;(t)}{\gamma(t)-p(1)+a-a}dt=\int_0^1\frac{\gamma&apos;(t)}{\gamma(t)-b}dt=\int_\gamma\frac{1}{z-b}dz=(2\pi i)n(\gamma;b)\]</p><p>Thus \(n(\gamma;a)=n(\gamma;b)\).</p></solution><topics><PointSetTopology/><ArgumentPrinciple/><CauchyIntegralTheorem/><WindingNumber/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
