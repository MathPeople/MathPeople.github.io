<problem><p>Let \(\Omega\) be a bounded domain in \(\mathbb{C}\). Say a holomorphic function \(f:\Omega\to\mathbb{C}\) is \(L^2\) if</p><p>\[\|f\|_2=\sqrt{\iint_\Omega|f(z)|^2dxdy}&lt;\infty\]</p><p>Let \(L^2(\Omega)\) be the set of \(L^2\) holomorphic functions defined on \(\Omega\). Show that \(L^2(\Omega)\) is an inner product space with inner product</p><p>\[\langle f,g\rangle=\iint_\Omega f(x+iy)\overline{g(x+iy)}dxdy\]</p><p>Show that for a fixed point \(a\in\Omega\), the assignment \(f\mapsto f(a)\) is a bounded linear operator on \(L^2(\Omega)\).</p></problem><solution><p>\(L^2(\Omega)\) is a vector space via the triangle inequality. Clearly the inner product is additive in both entries, linear in the first, conjugate linear in the second, conjugate symmetric, and positive semidefinite. It is positive definite because holomorphic functions are continuous.</p><p>Fix \(a\in\Omega\) and an \(r&gt;0\) such that \(\overline{B_r(a)}\subset\Omega\). Let \(f\in L^2(\Omega)\). By the ball-verision mean value property,</p><p>\[f(a)=\frac{1}{\pi r^2}\iint_{B_r(a)}f(z)dxdy\]</p><p>Therefore</p><p>\[|f(a)|\leq\frac{1}{\pi r^2}\iint_{B_r(a)}|f(z)|dxdy\leq\frac{1}{\pi r^2}\|f\|_1\]</p><p>Recall HÃ¶lder&apos;s inequality (special case for \(2\) norm),</p><p>\[\|fg\|_1\leq\|f\|_2\|g\|_2\]</p><p>Applying this with \(g=1\) gives</p><p>\[|f(a)|\leq\|f\|_1\leq\frac{\sqrt{\mu(\Omega)}}{\pi r^2}\|f\|_2\]</p><p>Thus evaluation at \(a\) is a bounded linear operator on \(L^2(\Omega)\) with bound at most \(\frac{\sqrt{\mu(\Omega)}}{\pi r^2}\).</p></solution><topics><IntegralEstimates/><FunctionBounds/><MeanValueProperty/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
