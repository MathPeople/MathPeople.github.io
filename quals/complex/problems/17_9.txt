<problem><p>Let \(R&gt;0\) and \(a\in\mathbb{C}\) have \(|a|&lt;R\). Evaluate</p><p>\[\int_{|z|=R}\frac{|dz|}{|z-a|^2}\]</p></problem><solution><p>The curve is given by \(Re^{it}\) for \(t\in[-\pi,\pi]\). Then \(|dz|=Rdt\) and the integral becomes</p><p>\[\int_{-\pi}^\pi\frac{R}{\left|Re^{it}-a\right|^2}dt\]</p><p>This is secretly a Dirichlet problem. We want the integrand to look like the Poisson kernel, which takes the form (see the proof of Harnack&apos;s inequality) </p><p>\[\frac{R^2-r^2}{\left|Re^{it}-re^{i\theta}\right|^2}\]</p><p> Thus we want to look at the integral </p><p>\[\int_{-\pi}^\pi\frac{R}{\left|Re^{it}-a\right|^2}dt=\frac{2\pi R}{R^2-r^2}\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\pi}^\pi\frac{R^2-r^2}{\left|Re^{it}-re^{i\theta}\right|^2}u(Re^{it})dt\]</p><p> where \(u=1\) and \(a=re^{i\theta}\). Because this is the Dirichlet solution, the integral (along with the \(\frac{1}{2\pi}\)) becomes \(u(a)=1\), so our answer is </p><p>\[\frac{2\pi R}{R^2-|a|^2}\]</p></solution><topics><DirichletProblem/></topics><instructors><a/><b/></instructors>
