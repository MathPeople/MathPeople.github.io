<problem><p>Given a Mobius transformation \(f(z)=\frac{az+b}{cz+d}\) give necessary and sufficient conditions on \(a,b,c,d\) so that \(f\) takes \(R=\{z:\Re(z)&gt;0\}\) to \(L=\{z:\Re(z)&lt;0\}\).</p></problem><solution partiallyFinished=""><p>Fix the Mobius transformation \(M\),</p><p>\[M(z)=\frac{z-1}{z+1}\]</p><p>which takes</p><p>\[0\mapsto-1\]</p><p>\[i\mapsto i\]</p><p>\[\infty\mapsto1\]</p><p>That is, \(M(R)=\mathbb{D}\). Note that \(M\) also takes \(\mathbb{D}\) to \(L\) because</p><p>\[M(1)=0\]</p><p>\[M(i)=i\]</p><p>\[M(-1)=\infty\]</p><p>If we can classify all Mobius transformations which fix the unit disc then precomposing and postcomposing with \(M\) gives all possibilities for \(f\).</p><p>We know that automorphisms of the disc take the form</p><p>\[\frac{\alpha z+\beta}{\overline\beta z+\overline\alpha}\]</p><p>where \(\alpha,\beta\) are complex numbers with \(|\alpha|^2-|\beta|^2=1\). Precomposing with \(M\) gives</p><p>\[\frac{\alpha\frac{z-1}{z+1}+\beta}{\overline\beta\frac{z-1}{z+1}+\overline\alpha}=\frac{\alpha(z-1)+\beta(z+1)}{\overline\beta(z-1)+\overline\alpha(z+1)}=\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+\beta-\alpha}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}\]</p><p>Postcomposing with \(M\) gives</p><p>\[\frac{\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+\beta-\alpha}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}-1}{\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+\beta-\alpha}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}+1}=\frac{\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+\beta-\alpha}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}-\frac{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}}{\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+\beta-\alpha}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}+\frac{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}{\overline{(\alpha+\beta)}z+\overline{(\alpha-\beta)}}}\]</p><p>\[=\frac{(\alpha+\beta)z+(\beta-\alpha)-(\overline{\alpha+\beta})z+(\overline{\beta-\alpha})}{(\alpha+\beta)z+(\beta-\alpha)+(\overline{\alpha+\beta})z-(\overline{\beta-\alpha})}\]</p><p>We can multiply top and bottom by 2 and set this equal to \(f\) to obtain</p><p>\[a=i\Im(\alpha+\beta)\]</p><p>\[b=\Re(\beta-\alpha)\]</p><p>\[c=\Re(\alpha+\beta)\]</p><p>\[d=i\Im(\beta-\alpha)\]</p><p>These are necessary and sufficient conditions, though they can probably be simplified to a nicer form.</p></solution><topics><MobiusTransformation/></topics><instructors><c/><f/></instructors>
