<problem><p>Does there exist an analytic function \(f:\mathbb{D}\to\mathbb{D}\) with \(f\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)=\frac{3}{4}\) and \(f&apos;\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)=\frac{2}{3}\)?</p></problem><solution><p>For any \(a\in\mathbb{D}\) recall \(\phi_a\) defined as</p><p>\[\phi_a(z)=\frac{z-a}{1-\overline az}\]</p><p>is an automorphism of \(\mathbb{D}\). Moreover \(\phi&apos;(0)=1-|a|^2\) and \(\phi&apos;(a)=\frac{1}{1-|a|^2}\). Define \(g:\mathbb{D}\to\mathbb{D}\) as</p><p>\[g=\phi_{\frac{3}{4}}\circ f\circ\phi_{\frac{-1}{2}}\]</p><p>Then \(|g&apos;(0)|\leq1\) by Schwarz&apos; lemma. That is,</p><p>\[\left|g&apos;(0)\right|=\left|\phi_{\frac{3}{4}}&apos;\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)\right|\left|f&apos;\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)\right|\left|\phi_{\frac{-1}{2}}&apos;(0)\right|\]</p><p>\[=\frac{1}{1-\frac{9}{16}}\left|f&apos;(0)\right|\left(1-\frac{1}{4}\right)=\frac{\frac{3}{4}}{\frac{7}{16}}\left|f&apos;(0)\right|=\frac{12}{7}\left|f&apos;(0)\right|\leq1\]</p><p>This means \(|f&apos;(0)|\leq\frac{7}{12}&lt;\frac{8}{12}=\frac{2}{3}\). Thus the answer is no.</p></solution><topics><FunctionBounds/><MobiusTransformation/><Schwarz/></topics><instructors><c/><f/></instructors>
