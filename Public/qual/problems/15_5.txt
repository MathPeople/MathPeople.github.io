<problem><p>Prove that a locally bounded family of analytic functions is equicontinuous on compact subsets of the domain in question. You cannot use Montel&apos;s theorem.</p></problem><solution><p>Suppose that \(\mathcal{F}\) is locally bounded at all points in \(\Omega\). Take the constants \(M,r\) from the definition of locally bounded, also choose \(r\) small enough so that \(\overline{B_r(z_0)}\subset\Omega\). That is, for all \(z\in B_r(z_0)\) and all \(f\in\mathcal{F}\), we have asusmed that \(|f(z)|\leq M\).</p><p>Let \(\epsilon&gt;0\) be given. Let \(z\in\Omega\) with \(|z-z_0|&lt;\frac{r}{2}\). Let \(\gamma\) be the circle \(|z-z_0|=r\). By Cauchy&apos;s integral formula applied to arbitrary \(f\in\mathcal{F}\), </p><p>\[|f(z)-f(z_0)|=\left|\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma\frac{f(w)}{w-z}dw-\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma\frac{f(w)}{w-z_0}dw\right|=\frac{1}{2\pi}\left|\int_\gamma\frac{f(w)(z-z_0)}{(w-z)(w-z_0)}dw\right|\]</p><p> </p><p>\[\leq\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_\gamma\left|\frac{f(w)(z-z_0)}{(w-z)(w-z_0)}\right||dw|\]</p><p> Here we will use some bounds. \(|f(w)|\leq M\), \(|w-z_0|=r\), and \(|w-z|\geq\frac{r}{2}\). Thus </p><p>\[\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_\gamma\left|f(w)(z-z_0)\frac{1}{w-z}\frac{1}{w-z_0}\right||dw|\leq\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_\gamma M|z-z_0|\frac{2}{r}\frac{1}{r}|dw|=\frac{M}{\pi r^2}|z-z_0|\int_\gamma|dw|=\frac{2M}{r}|z-z_0|\]</p><p> Therefore if we choose \(\delta\) to be \(0&lt;\delta&lt;\frac{r}{2}\) (so this whole argument applies) and less than \(\frac{r\epsilon}{2M}\), then we have shown that \(\mathcal{F}\) is equicontinuous because </p><p>\[|f(z)-f(z_0)|\leq\frac{2M}{r}\delta&lt;\frac{2M}{r}\frac{r\epsilon}{2M}=\epsilon\]</p></solution><topics><IntegralEstimates/><CauchyIntegralFormula/><FunctionBounds/><SequenceOfFunctions/><Montel/></topics><instructors><a/><c/></instructors>
