<problem><p>Let \(f\) be an analytic function on a region containing \(\{z\in\mathbb{C}:|z|\leq4\}\). Suppose that \(|f(z)|\leq1\) whenever \(|z|\leq4\). Prove that \(|f(z_1)-f(z_2)|\leq\frac{8}{9}\) whenever \(|z_1|,|z_2|\leq1\).</p></problem><solution><p>By Cauchy&apos;s integral formula,</p><p>\[|f(z_1)-f(z_2)|=\left|\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{|z|=4}\frac{f(w)}{(w-z_1)}-\frac{f(w)}{(w-z_2)}dw\right|=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{|z|=4}\left|\frac{f(w)(z_2-z_1)}{(w-z_1)(w-z_2)}\right||dw|\]</p><p>We know \(|f(w)|\leq1\), \(|z_2-z_1|\leq|z_2|+|z_1|&lt;2\), and for \(j=1,2\) that \(|w-z_j|\geq|w|-|z_j|&gt;4-1=3\). Thus </p><p>\[\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{|z|=4}\left|\frac{f(w)(z_2-z_1)}{(w-z_1)(w-z_2)}\right||dw|\leq\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{|z|=4}\frac{1\cdot2}{3\cdot3}|dw|=\frac{1}{2\pi}\cdot\frac{2}{9}\cdot8\pi=\frac{8}{9}\]</p></solution><topics><CauchyIntegralFormula/><IntegralEstimates/><FunctionBounds/></topics><instructors><a/><b/></instructors>
