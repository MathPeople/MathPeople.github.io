<problem><p>Find the Laurent series of \(f(z)=\frac{1}{z(z-1)(3z-2)}\) inside the annulus \(\frac{1}{6}&lt;\left|z-\frac{1}{2}\right|&lt;\frac{1}{2}\).</p></problem><solution><p>By partial fraction decomposition, this function can be written as,</p><p>\[f(z)=\frac{1}{2z}+\frac{1}{z-1}+\frac{-9}{2(3z-2)}\]</p><p>We can rewrite,  </p><p>\[\frac{1}{2z}+\frac{1}{z-1}+\frac{-9}{2(3z-2)}=\frac{1}{2(z-\frac{1}{2})+1}+\frac{-2}{-2(z-\frac{1}{2})+1}+\frac{-9}{2(3z-2)}\]</p><p>Whenever, \(\left| z-\frac{1}{2} \right| &lt; \frac{1}{2}\), the first two terms have an expansion of,</p><p>\[\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{n} 2^{n} \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)^{n}-2 \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2^{n} \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)^{n}\]</p><p>Now on, </p><p>\[\left| z-\frac{1}{2} \right|&gt; \frac{1}{6}\]</p><p>We have that, </p><p>\[\frac{-9}{2(3z-2)}=\frac{\frac{-3}{2}}{\left(z-\frac{1}{2} \right)\left(1-\frac{1}{6 (z-\frac{1}{2})} \right)}\]</p><p>The Laurent series is thus,</p><p>\[\frac{-3}{2 \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 6^{-n} \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)^{-n}=\frac{-3}{2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 6^{-n} \left( z- \frac{1}{2} \right)^{-n-1}\]</p><p>Therefore, summing up, the Laurent series for this function is,</p><p>\[\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{n} 2^{n} \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)^{n}- \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2^{n+1} \left( z-\frac{1}{2} \right)^{n} -\frac{3}{2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 6^{-n} \left( z- \frac{1}{2} \right)^{-n-1}\]</p></solution><topics><SumConvergence/><RadiusOfConvergence/><Laurent/></topics>
