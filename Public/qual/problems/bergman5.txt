<problem><p>Recall \(K(z,z)&gt;0\) on any bounded domain. Thus \(\ln K(z,z)\) makes sense. Specifically for the disc, compute</p><p>\[\frac{d^2}{dzd\overline z}\ln K(z,z)\]</p><p>Recall that \(\frac{d}{dz}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d}{dx}+i\frac{d}{dy}\right)\) and that \(\frac{d}{d\overline z}=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{d}{dx}-i\frac{d}{dy}\right)\).</p></problem><solution><p>We computed \(K(z,z)\) on the disc to be</p><p>\[K(z,z)=\frac{1}{\pi\left(1-|z|^2\right)^2}=\frac{1}{\pi}\left(1-z\overline z\right)^{-2}\]</p><p>Then</p><p>\[\ln K(z,z)=-\ln\pi-2\ln(1-z\overline z)\]</p><p>Taking \(\frac{d}{dz}\) of this gives</p><p>\[2\frac{\overline z}{1-z\overline z}\]</p><p>Taking \(\frac{d}{d\overline z}\) of this gives</p><p>\[2\frac{1-z\overline z+z\overline z}{\left(1-z\overline z\right)^2}=\frac{2}{\left(1-|z|^2\right)^2}\]</p></solution><topics><DifferentialForms/><Conjugation/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
