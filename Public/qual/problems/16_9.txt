<problem><p>Find the Laurent series of the function \(f(z)=\frac{z-1}{(z-2)(z-3)}\) for the annulus \(2&lt;|z|&lt;3\).</p></problem><solution><p>Recall that, for \(|r|&lt;1\),</p><p>\[\sum_{n=0}^\infty r^n=\frac{1}{1-r}\]</p><p>If \(2&lt;|z|\) then \(\left|\frac{2}{z}\right|&lt;1\), so </p><p>\[\sum_{n=0}^\infty\left(\frac{2}{z}\right)^n=\frac{1}{1-\frac{2}{z}}=\frac{z}{z-2}\]</p><p> If \(|z|&lt;3\) then \(\left|\frac{z}{3}\right|&lt;1\), so </p><p>\[\sum_{n=0}^\infty\left(\frac{z}{3}\right)^n=\frac{1}{1-\frac{z}{3}}=\frac{-3}{z-3}\]</p><p> Now </p><p>\[\frac{z-1}{(z-2)(z-3)}=(z-1)\frac{1}{-3z}\frac{-3z}{(z-2)(z-3)}=\frac{z-1}{-3z}\left(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\left(\frac{2}{z}\right)^n\right)\left(\sum_{n=0}^\infty\left(\frac{z}{3}\right)^n\right)\]</p><p> Further simplification is possible, but unnecessary. We have a Laurent expansion which converges in the correct region.</p></solution><topics><RadiusOfConvergence/><SumConvergence/><Laurent/></topics><instructors><a/><c/></instructors>
