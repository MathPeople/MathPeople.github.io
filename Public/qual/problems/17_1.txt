<problem><p>Let \(a&gt;0\). Find</p><p>\[\int_0^\infty\frac{\cos(ax)}{1+x^2}dx\]</p></problem><solution><p>Let \(f\) be the function</p><p>\[f(z)=\frac{e^{iaz}}{1+z^2}=\frac{\cos(az)+i\sin(az)}{1+z^2}\]</p><p>If \(z\in\mathbb{R}\), then this is mostly what we are looking for. That is, </p><p>\[\int_\mathbb{R}f(z)dz=\int_{-\infty}^\infty\frac{\cos(ax)}{1+x^2}+i\frac{\sin(ax)}{1+x^2}dx\]</p><p> Moreover, \(\sin(ax)\) is an odd function and \(\cos(ax)\) is an even function. Thus </p><p>\[\int_0^\infty\frac{\cos(ax)}{1+x^2}dx=\frac{1}{2}\int_\mathbb{R}f(z)dz\]</p><p> To find this, let&apos;s instead try to solve \(\int_\gamma f(z)dz\) where \(\gamma\) is the boundary of the upper half disc of radius \(R\) centered at the origin. \(f\) is a meromorphic function on \(\mathbb{C}\) with poles only at \(\pm i\). As long as \(R&gt;1\), Cauchy&apos;s integral formula tells us that </p><p>\[\int_\gamma f(z)dz=\int_\gamma\frac{e^{iaz}/(z+i)}{z-i}dz=2\pi ie^{ai^2}/(i+i)=\pi e^{-a}\]</p><p> Now we just need to estimate the arc portion of \(\gamma\). Let \(p\) be the curved portion of \(\gamma\). Then \(p(t)=Re^{it}\) for \(t\in[0,\pi]\). An estimate for \(f\circ p(t)\) is </p><p>\[|f(p(t))|=\left|\frac{1}{1+R^2e^{2it}}e^{iaRe^{it}}\right|\leq\frac{1}{R^2-1}\left|e^{iaR(\cos(t)+i\sin(t)}\right|\]</p><p> </p><p>\[=\frac{1}{R^2-1}\left|e^{-aR\sin(t)+iaR\cos(t)}\right|=\frac{1}{R^2-1}e^{-aR\sin(t)}\]</p><p> Because \(0\leq t\leq\pi\), we know that \(\sin(t)\) is nonnegative. Then \(-aR\sin(t)\leq0\), so \(e^{-aR\sin(t)}\leq1\). Thus, on \(p\), </p><p>\[|f(p(t))|\leq\frac{1}{R^2-1}\]</p><p> Then the integral is bounded by </p><p>\[\left|\int_pf(z)dz\right|\leq\int_p|f(z)||dz|=\int_0^\pi|f(p(t))|Rdt\leq\int_0^\pi\frac{1}{R^2-1}Rdt=\frac{R\pi}{R^2-1}\to0\]</p><p> That is, the contribution from \(p\) vanishes as \(R\to\infty\). Thus </p><p>\[2\int_0^\infty\frac{\cos(ax)}{1+x^2}dx=\int_\mathbb{R}f(z)dz=\lim_{R\to\infty}\int_\gamma f(z)dz=\pi e^{-a}\]</p><p> So our answer is \(\frac{\pi}{2}e^{-a}\)</p></solution><topics><IntegralEstimates/><FunctionBounds/><RealIntegral/><CauchyIntegralFormula/><ExpAndTrig/></topics><instructors><a/><b/></instructors>
