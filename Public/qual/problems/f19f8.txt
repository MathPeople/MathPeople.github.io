<problem><p>Let \(f\) be an entire function and let \(a,b\in\mathbb{C}\) with \(|a|&lt;R\) and \(|b|&lt;R\). If \(\gamma(t)=Re^{it}, 0\leq t\leq2\pi\), evaluate</p><p>\[\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{(z-a)(z-b)}dz.\]</p><p>Use this result to give another proof of Liouville&apos;s Theorem.</p></problem><solution><p>First we rewrite the function \(\frac{f(z)}{(z-a)(z-b)}\) as \(f(z)(a-b)\left(\frac{1}{z-a}-\frac{1}{z-b}\right)\) to justify evaluating the integral(s)</p><p>\[\int_\gamma f(z)(z-a)\left(\frac{1}{a-b}-\frac{1}{z-b}\right)dz=(a-b)\left(\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{z-a}dz-\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{z-b}dz\right)\]</p><p>By Cauchy&apos;s integral formula these yield</p><p>\[=(a-b)(f(a)-f(b))\]</p><p>On the other hand, an estimate gives</p><p>\[\left|\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{(z-a)(z-b)}dz\right|\leq\int_\gamma\frac{|f(z)|}{|z-a||z-b|}|dz|\]</p><p>Suppose \(f\) is globally bounded by \(M\) in modulus. Then</p><p>\[|(a-b)(f(a)-f(b))|\leq\int_\gamma\frac{M}{|z-a||z-b|}|dz|\]</p><p>\[\leq\int_\gamma\frac{M}{(|z|-|a|)(|z|-|b|)}|dz|=\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{M}{(R-|a|)(R-|b|)}Rdt=\frac{2\pi RM}{(R-|a|)(R-|b|)}\]</p><p>Now for the proof structure. Let \(a,b\in\mathbb{C}\) and suppose \(R&gt;|a|+|b|\). Then, by the above calculations,</p><p>\[|f(a)-f(b)|\leq\frac{2\pi RM}{|a-b|(R-|a|)(R-|b|)}\]</p><p>Taking the limit as \(R\to\infty\) (which we can do because \(f\) is entire) shows \(f(a)=f(b)\), which is only possible for all \(a,b\in\mathbb{C}\) if \(f\) is constant.</p></solution><topics><CauchyIntegralFormula/><IntegralEstimates/><FunctionBounds/><EntireFunction/><Liouville/></topics><instructors><d/></instructors>
