<problem><p>Prove that a harmonic function \(u\) on \(\Omega\) satisfies the mean value property, which we rename the circle mean value property. Prove also that \(u\) satisfies the ball mean value property that for all \(a\in\Omega\) and all \(r&gt;0\) small enough that \(\overline{B_r(a)}\subset\Omega\)</p><p>\[u(a)=\frac{1}{\pi r^2}\iint_{B_r(a)}u(x+iy)dxdy\]</p></problem><solution><p>By choice of \(a,r\) there is a simply connected open subset of \(\Omega\) which contains \(\overline{B_r(a)}\). Take a harmonic conjugate \(v\) on this neighborhood to obtain the holomorphic function \(f=u+iv\). Recall Cauchy&apos;s integral formula,</p><p>\[f(a)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{|z-a|=r}\frac{f(z)}{z-a}dz\]</p><p>Restating with \(u\) and \(v\) gives</p><p>\[u(a)+iv(a)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{u(a+re^{it})+iv(a+re^{it})}{re^{it}}ire^{it}dt=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}u(a+re^{it})+iv(a+re^{it})dt\]</p><p>The real part of the above equation is the circle mean value property.</p><p>Let \(0&lt;s&lt;r\). Because \(u\) satisfies the circle mean value property,</p><p>\[u(a)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}u(a+se^{i\theta})d\theta\]</p><p>Scale both sides by \(s\).</p><p>\[2\pi su(a)=\int_0^{2\pi}u(a+se^{i\theta})sd\theta\]</p><p>Integrate both sides with respect to \(s\) from \(0\) to \(r\).</p><p>\[\pi r^2u(a)=\int_0^r\int_0^{2\pi}u(a+se^{i\theta})sd\theta ds=\iint_{B_r(a)}u(x+iy)dxdy\]</p></solution><topics><CauchyIntegralFormula/><RealIntegral/><MeanValueProperty/><Harmonic/><HarmonicConjugate/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
