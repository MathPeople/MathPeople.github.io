<problem><p>We say a holomorphic function \(f\) defined on a region \(\Omega\) is \(L^2\) if</p><p>\[\|f\|_2=\sqrt{\iint_\Omega|f(x+iy)|^2dxdy}&lt;\infty\]</p><p>Suppose \(\{f_n\}\) is a sequence of \(L^2\) holomorphic functions on \(\Omega\) and each \(\|f_n\|_2&lt;M\) for some \(0&lt;M&lt;\infty\). Show that there is a subsequence \(m\) of \(n\) and a holomorphic function \(f:\Omega\to\mathbb{C}\) such that</p><p>\[f_m\to f\]</p><p>uniformly on compact sets.</p></problem><solution><p>We show that \(f_n\) is bounded for each \(n\). Let \(a\in\Omega\). Then there is a constant \(C\) depending on \(\Omega\) such that</p><p>\[|f(a)|\leq C\|f\|_2\leq CM\]</p><p>Thus \(f_n\) is bounded, so locally bounded, so the result follows from applying Monte&apos;s theorem.</p></solution><topics><FunctionBounds/><Montel/><SequenceOfFunctions/></topics><instructors><e/></instructors>
