<problem><p>Let \(f\) be an entire function such that \(|f(z)|=|f(\frac{1}{z})|\) for all nonzero \(z\). Prove that \(f\) is constant.</p></problem><solution><p>Because \(f\) is continuous, \(f\left(\overline{\mathbb{D}}\right)\) is compact and therefore bounded, let \(M\) be a bound. For any \(|z|&gt;1, \frac{1}{z}\in D\) so \(|f(z)|=|f\left(\frac{1}{z}\right)|\leq M\). Therefore \(f\) is a bounded entire function which, by Liouville&apos;s theorem, must be constant.</p></solution><topics><FunctionBounds/><PointSetTopology/><Liouville/></topics><instructors><c/><a/></instructors>
