<problem><p>State and prove Liouville&apos;s theorem.</p></problem><solution><p>Theorem: a bounded entire function must be constant. In other words, if \(f:\mathbb{C}\to\mathbb{C}\) is bounded \((|f(z)|\leq M\forall z\in\mathbb{C})\) and holomorphic then \(f\) is constant.</p><p>	</p><p>	Proof: use Cauchy&apos;s estimate (just the Cauchy integral formula).</p><p>	</p><p>	Let \(z_0\in\mathbb{C}\). We will show \(f&apos;(z_0)=0\). This is for all points, so \(f\) must then be constant.</p><p>	</p><p>	Let \(r&gt;0\) and let \(\gamma\) be the boundary of the ball centered at \(z_0\) of radius \(r\), \(\gamma(\theta)=z_0+re^{i\theta}\). Because \(f\) is entire, \(f\) is holomorphic on the ball. By Cauchy&apos;s integral formula (extended version with derivatives),</p><p>	</p><p>\[f&apos;(z_0)=\frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^2}dz\]</p><p>	</p><p>	Taking absolute value gives</p><p>	</p><p>\[|f&apos;(z_0)|=\frac{1}{2\pi}\left|\int_\gamma\frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^2}dz\right|\leq\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_\gamma\frac{|f(z)|}{|z-z_0|^2}|dz|\]</p><p>	</p><p>\[=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{|f(z_0+re^{i\theta})|}{|z_0+re^{i\theta}-z_0|^2}|ire^{i\theta}d\theta|=\frac{1}{2\pi r}\int_0^{2\pi}|f(z_0+re^{i\theta})|d\theta\]</p><p>	</p><p>	By assumption, \(|f(z_0+re^{i\theta})|\leq M\), so</p><p>	</p><p>\[|f&apos;(z_0)|\leq\frac{1}{2\pi r}\int_0^{2\pi}Md\theta=\frac{M}{r}\]</p><p>	</p><p>	This is true for any \(r&gt;0\) because \(f\) is entire. Thus \(|f&apos;(z_0)|\leq0\), so \(f&apos;=0\) as claimed.</p></solution><topics><CauchyIntegralFormula/><IntegralEstimates/><FunctionBounds/><EntireFunction/><CauchyEstimate/><Liouville/></topics><instructors><c/></instructors>
