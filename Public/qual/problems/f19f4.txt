<problem><p>Let \(f\) be analytic in the unit disc \(\mathbb{D}=B_1(0)\) and suppose that \(|f(z)|\leq1\) for all \(|z|&lt;1\). Show that \(|f&apos;(0)|\leq1\).</p></problem><solution><p>We could probably get this to work using Schwarz&apos; lemma, but why not use Cauchy&apos;s estimate instead? For a refresher, if \(|f(z)|\leq M\) for all \(|z|&lt;R\) then</p><p>\[|f^{k}(0)|\leq\frac{Mk!}{R^k}\]</p><p>Applying this to our situation where \(M=k=R=1\) gives \(|f&apos;(0)|\leq1\).</p></solution><topics><FunctionBounds/><CauchyEstimate/><Schwarz/></topics><instructors><d/></instructors>
