<problem><p>Is there a sequence of polynomials which converge uniformly to the function \(\frac{1}{z}\) on the annulus \(1&lt;|z|&lt;2\)?</p></problem><solution><p>No. Let \(G\) be the annulus \(1&lt;|z|&lt;2\). Suppose \(f:G\to\mathbb{C}\) is analytic and \(p_n\to f\) uniformly on \(G\). Let \(c\) be once around the circle \(|z|=\frac{3}{2}\). Because \(p_n\) are entire functions for all \(n\), by the Cauchy integral theorem</p><p>\[\int_cp_ndz=0\]</p><p>Then, by uniform convergence, </p><p>\[\lim\int_cp_ndz=\int_c\lim p_ndz=\int_cfdz=0\]</p><p> Thus \(f(z)\neq\frac{1}{z}\) because </p><p>\[\int_c\frac{1}{z}dz=2\pi i\neq0\]</p></solution><topics><ResidueTheorem/><CauchyIntegralTheorem/><SumConvergence/></topics><instructors><a/><b/></instructors>
