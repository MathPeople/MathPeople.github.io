<problem><p>Find the Laurent series of \(f(z)=\frac{1}{(z-1)(z-2)}\) in the annulus \(1&lt;|z|&lt;2\).</p></problem><solution><p>The partial fraction decomposition of the function becomes,</p><p>\[f(z)=\frac{-1}{z-1}+\frac{1}{z-2}=\frac{1}{1-z}-\frac{1}{2(1-\frac{z}{2})}\]</p><p>When \(|z|&gt;1\),</p><p>\[\frac{1}{1-z}=\frac{1}{z(1-\frac{1}{z})}\]</p><p>Therefore, the Laurent series is,</p><p>\[=\frac{1}{z(1-\frac{1}{z})}-\frac{1}{2(1-\frac{z}{2})}\]</p><p>\[=\frac{1}{z} \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} z^{-i}-\frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=0}^{\infty} 2^{-i} z^{i}\]</p></solution><topics><SumConvergence/><RadiusOfConvergence/><Laurent/></topics>
